All About Cheetahs

The world's fastest land animal

Habitat

Cheetahs are commonly found in the African savannas where they roam freely and hunt in open landscapes.

Speed

Cheetahs can run at speeds of up to 120 km/h, making them the fastest land animals, capable of short bursts of incredible acceleration.

Diet

They primarily hunt small to medium-sized ungulates, relying on their agility and speed to catch their prey.

Behavior

Cheetahs are solitary animals, although females tend to live with their cubs for up to two years. Males sometimes form small groups called coalitions.

Reproduction

Female cheetahs usually give birth to a litter of 3-5 cubs after a gestation period of about 3 months. Cubs are vulnerable to predators and rely on their mother's protection.

Threats

Cheetahs face threats from habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict, and illegal wildlife trade. Their population is declining, and they are classified as vulnerable.
